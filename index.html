Self Development

My self‑development is shaped by both my thoughts and emotions, and how they guide the way I see myself and respond to the world. Looking back, I’ve learned trust from childhood, independence as I grew older, and identity through my teenage years. Now, at 19, I’m starting to build deeper connections and learning the value of intimacy and support.

I know I’m not perfect — sometimes I feel isolated or unsure — but I use those moments to grow. My coping mechanisms, like turning stress into creativity or finding comfort in supportive people, help me stay balanced. For me, the psychological self is about understanding how my inner drives, habits, and emotions connect with my values and relationships. It’s not separate from my philosophical or socio‑anthropological self — it’s all part of the same journey of becoming.
